Stage 10-3-2 · Modulation by a Complex Sinusoid 關卡 10-3-2 · 複正弦調變 SASP §10.3.2 ↗

Review the modulation theorem as it is used in the DFT filter bank: multiplying a sequence by $e^{j\omega_c n}$ shifts its entire DTFT by $\omega_c$. With this single identity, we convert lowpass ↔ bandpass at will and connect the prototype rectangular filter to the $N$ bandpass channels of the DFT FB.

複習調變定理在 DFT 濾波器組中的用法：序列乘上 $e^{j\omega_c n}$ 就把整個 DTFT 位移 $\omega_c$。只靠這一條恆等式，我們就能自由切換低通↔帶通，並把原型矩形濾波器連上 DFT FB 的 $N$ 個帶通通道。

The modulation theorem (discrete-time)

調變定理（離散時間版）

SHIFT IN FREQUENCY
$$x[n]\,e^{j\omega_c n}\;\longleftrightarrow\;X(\omega - \omega_c)$$

Multiplying a sequence by a complex exponential in the time domain slides its spectrum rigidly by $\omega_c$ in the frequency domain. The shape of $|X|$ stays identical — only its centre moves.

在時域將序列乘上複指數，等同於頻域把整個頻譜剛性位移 $\omega_c$。$|X|$ 的形狀不變，只有中心搬家。

Proof in one line

一行證明

$$\mathcal{F}\{x[n]\,e^{j\omega_c n}\}\;=\;\sum_n x[n]\,e^{j\omega_c n}\,e^{-j\omega n}\;=\;\sum_n x[n]\,e^{-j(\omega-\omega_c)n}\;=\;X(\omega-\omega_c)$$

Using it: turn an $N$-sample lowpass into a bandpass

應用：把 $N$-樣本低通變成帶通

Take the running-sum lowpass $h_{\text{RS}}[n]$ (a rectangle from §10.3.1). Multiply by $e^{j\omega_k n}$ with $\omega_k=2\pi k/N$:

取 §10.3.1 的累計和低通 $h_{\text{RS}}[n]$（矩形）。乘上 $e^{j\omega_k n}$，其中 $\omega_k=2\pi k/N$：

CHANNEL k IMPULSE RESP.
$$h_k[n]\;=\;h_{\text{RS}}[n]\,e^{j\omega_k n}\;\longleftrightarrow\;H_{\text{RS}}(\omega-\omega_k)$$

A lowpass centred at $0$ becomes a bandpass centred at $\omega_k$, with exactly the same magnitude shape. Because $\omega_k$ is chosen to be $2\pi k/N$, adjacent channels butt up at the Dirichlet sinc's spectral nulls: channel $k-1$'s null coincides with channel $k$'s peak.

中心在 $0$ 的低通變成中心在 $\omega_k$ 的帶通，振幅形狀完全不變。因為 $\omega_k$ 刻意取在 $2\pi k/N$，相鄰通道正好在 Dirichlet sinc 的零點上對齊：第 $k-1$ 通道的零點恰好是第 $k$ 通道的峰。

Why complex exponentials, not cosines?

為什麼用複指數而非餘弦？

Real-valued modulation $x[n]\cos(\omega_c n)$ would split the spectrum into two copies at $\pm\omega_c$ — fine for AM radio, bad for a filter bank because now each "band" has two lobes. Complex exponentials produce a single one-sided shift, which is what the DFT-FB view needs so each $k$ maps to exactly one channel. That's why the DFT uses $e^{-j\omega_k n}$ and not $\cos(\omega_k n)$.

實值調變 $x[n]\cos(\omega_c n)$ 會把頻譜劈成 $\pm\omega_c$ 兩份 — 對 AM 無線電 OK，對濾波器組不好，因為每個「頻帶」會長兩個波瓣。複指數只做單邊位移，這就是 DFT-FB 觀點所需 — 每個 $k$ 恰好對應一個通道。DFT 用 $e^{-j\omega_k n}$、不用 $\cos(\omega_k n)$ 的原因就在此。

Modulation is the FB's secret weapon. With one identity ($x e^{j\omega_c n} \leftrightarrow X(\omega-\omega_c)$) we promote a single prototype lowpass to $N$ equally-spaced bandpasses. The DFT FB uses the rectangle; Hann/Hamming/Blackman FBs (§10.4+) use the same modulation with a nicer prototype.

調變是 FB 的祕密武器。靠一條恆等式 $x e^{j\omega_c n} \leftrightarrow X(\omega-\omega_c)$，就把一個原型低通升級成 $N$ 個等距帶通。DFT FB 用矩形；Hann/Hamming/Blackman FB（§10.4+）把原型換成更漂亮的視窗、同樣靠這條調變。
