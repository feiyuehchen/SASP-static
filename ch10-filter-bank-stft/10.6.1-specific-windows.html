Why Hamming is "approximately COLA"

為何 Hamming 是「近似 COLA」

Hamming is $0.54 - 0.46\cos(2\pi n/M)$ — almost a Hann shape but with a DC bias of $0.08$ making the edges non-zero. The DC bias breaks strict COLA: $\sum_m w[n-mR] = c_0 + \epsilon\cos(\ldots)$ where $\epsilon \approx 0.01$. Audible only as about $-40$ dB ripple — inaudible in practice. So Hamming is used at $R=M/2$ in most audio pipelines despite not being exactly COLA.

Hamming：$0.54 - 0.46\cos(2\pi n/M)$ — 形狀接近 Hann 但直流偏置 $0.08$ 讓兩端非零。直流偏置破壞嚴格 COLA：$\sum_m w[n-mR] = c_0 + \epsilon\cos(\ldots)$，$\epsilon \approx 0.01$。實際上只出現 $-40$ dB 漣波，聽不出來。所以多數音訊流水線仍用 Hamming + $R=M/2$。

Kaiser as a tunable trade-off

Kaiser 作為可調的取捨

Kaiser window $w[n] \propto I_0(\beta\sqrt{1-(2n/M-1)^2})$ with shape parameter $\beta$. Larger $\beta$ = longer main lobe, lower side-lobes. For a target side-lobe level $A$ in dB, Kaiser gives $\beta \approx 0.1102(A-8.7)$. COLA is only approximate; in practice use it at hops where the approximation residual is below your noise floor.

Kaiser 視窗 $w[n] \propto I_0(\beta\sqrt{1-(2n/M-1)^2})$，以形狀參數 $\beta$ 控制。$\beta$ 越大 → 主波瓣越寬、旁波瓣越低。目標旁波瓣 $A$ dB 時，$\beta \approx 0.1102(A-8.7)$。COLA 只是近似；實務跳距要取到「近似殘差低於雜訊底」的程度。

Reality check — most audio uses Hann @ $R=M/2$

實務檢查 — 大多音訊用 Hann + $R=M/2$

If you just need "something that works": Hann at 50% overlap. Exactly COLA, simple to code, side-lobe $-32$ dB is good enough for SMS, time-stretch, harmoniser, and spectral mixing.

若你只要「能用的預設」：Hann + 50% 重疊。嚴格 COLA、好寫、旁波瓣 $-32$ dB 足以應付 SMS、時間伸縮、harmoniser、頻譜混音。

Pick the window first, then let COLA dictate the hop. Don't invert: if you insist on a specific hop, you constrain your window choice. Side-lobe requirements drive the window; COLA then snaps the hop to $M/\{2,3,4,\ldots\}$.

先挑視窗，讓 COLA 決定跳距。不要反過來：若堅持某個跳距，就把視窗選項框死。旁波瓣需求決定視窗；COLA 再把跳距 snap 到 $M/\{2,3,4,\ldots\}$。
