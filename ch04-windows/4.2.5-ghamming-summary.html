Three windows, one knob $\alpha$. The table below is the whole family.

三個視窗，一個旋鈕 $\alpha$。下表就是整個家族。

2-TERM COSINE WINDOWS
$$\begin{array}{llcc} \text{Name} & \alpha & \text{1st SL} & \text{Roll-off} \\ \text{Rectangular} & 1 & -13.3\ \text{dB} & 6\ \text{dB/oct} \\ \text{Hann} & 0.5 & -31.5\ \text{dB} & 18\ \text{dB/oct} \\ \text{Hamming} & 0.54 & -42.7\ \text{dB} & 6\ \text{dB/oct} \\ \end{array}$$

Main-lobe width is $8\pi/N$ for anything but pure rectangle. The trade is always: first side lobe vs. asymptotic roll-off. If you know where your interfering signals live, pick the window whose worst side lobe is lowest in that band.

除了純矩形外，主瓣寬都是 $8\pi/N$。取捨永遠是「第一旁瓣 vs. 漸進滾降」。若知道干擾訊號出現在哪一帶，就選在那一帶上最壞旁瓣最低的視窗。

When each one wins

三者各贏的情境

Rectangular: you want the narrowest possible main-lobe and don't mind $-13$ dB leakage (rare in audio).
Hann: the best asymptotic roll-off ($18$ dB/oct) — interfering signals far from your peak are best suppressed. Default for STFT/audio.
Hamming: lowest first side-lobe ($-43$ dB) at the cost of slower asymptotic decay — best when interference sits close to your peak. 矩形：要最窄的主瓣、不介意 $-13$ dB 洩漏（音訊少用）。
Hann：最佳漸進滾降（$18$ dB/oct）— 遠離峰的干擾壓制最好。STFT/音訊預設。
Hamming：第一旁瓣最低（$-43$ dB），代價是漸進衰減較慢 — 干擾接近峰時最適合。

One formula $w[n]=\alpha-(1-\alpha)\cos(\tfrac{2\pi n}{N-1})$, three engineering trade-offs. The 2-term cosine family is the simplest "tunable" window you can write down, and that simplicity is why it shows up so often.

一條公式 $w[n]=\alpha-(1-\alpha)\cos(\tfrac{2\pi n}{N-1})$、三種工程取捨。兩項餘弦家族是你能寫下最簡單的「可調」視窗，簡單正是它到處出現的原因。
