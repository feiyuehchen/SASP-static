Kaiser's design recipe: start with the target stopband attenuation $A$ in dB, read β from a closed-form formula.

Kaiser 設計配方：給定目標停帶衰減 $A$（dB），用閉式公式反推 β。

KAISER β FROM A (dB)
$$\beta=\begin{cases}0.1102(A-8.7),& A>50\\ 0.5842(A-21)^{0.4}+0.07886(A-21),& 21\le A\le 50\\ 0,& A<21\end{cases}$$

Quick lookup

查表

$$\begin{array}{cc} A\;\text{(dB)} & \beta \\ \hline 30 & 1.68 \\ 40 & 3.40 \\ 50 & 4.54 \\ 60 & 5.65 \\ 70 & 6.76 \\ 80 & 7.86 \\ 90 & 8.96 \\ 100 & 10.06 \\ 120 & 12.27 \\ \end{array}$$

For $A = 60$ dB → $\beta \approx 5.65$. For $A = 80$ dB → $\beta \approx 7.86$. Formula is accurate to a few tenths; just read the table if you want integer values.

$A = 60$ dB → $\beta \approx 5.65$；$A = 80$ dB → $\beta \approx 7.86$。公式誤差幾分之一；想要整數值直接查表。

Main-lobe width follows $\Delta \omega \approx (A - 8) / (2.285 N)$. Solve for $N$ to get the shortest Kaiser that meets both $A$ and $\Delta\omega$. Built into Matlab's kaiserord.

主瓣寬度 $\Delta \omega \approx (A - 8) / (2.285 N)$。解 $N$ 即得同時滿足 $A$ 與 $\Delta\omega$ 的最短 Kaiser。Matlab kaiserord 內建此計算。

Kaiser's design is "specify-and-compute". Tell it the attenuation you want, and the β (and optionally N) come out in closed form — no trial and error.

Kaiser 設計是「指定即得」。給定想要的衰減，β（以及 N）由閉式公式直出 — 無需試錯。
