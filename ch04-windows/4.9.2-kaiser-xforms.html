The DTFT of the continuous Kaiser is a scaled $\sinh$-ratio (not a plain sinc); in practice we always compute it numerically by FFT. Here we show three $\beta$ values side by side to see how the spectrum morphs.

連續 Kaiser 的 DTFT 是縮放的 $\sinh$ 比（非純 sinc）；實務一律以 FFT 算。這裡展示三個 $\beta$ 並排看頻譜如何變形。

CONTINUOUS KAISER TRANSFORM
$$W(\omega)\;\propto\;\frac{\sinh\!\Bigl(\sqrt{\beta^2-(T\omega/2)^2}\Bigr)}{\sqrt{\beta^2-(T\omega/2)^2}}$$

For $|T\omega/2| < \beta$ the spectrum is a hyperbolic sine ratio (broad positive bump). For $|T\omega/2| > \beta$ the argument goes imaginary and the $\sinh$ becomes $\sin$, producing the side-lobe oscillations. The transition at $|T\omega/2| = \beta$ marks the main-lobe edge.

$|T\omega/2| < \beta$ 時為雙曲 sinh 比（正向寬凸起）。$|T\omega/2| > \beta$ 時變為虛引數，$\sinh$ 變 $\sin$，產生旁瓣振盪。$|T\omega/2| = \beta$ 處就是主瓣邊緣。

Kaiser's spectrum has one "shape" parametrised by β. β small ≈ Dirichlet sinc; β large ≈ very concentrated lobe. The $\sinh / \sin$ transition is what you see in the dB plot.

Kaiser 頻譜只有一種「形狀」，由 β 參數化。β 小 ≈ Dirichlet sinc；β 大 ≈ 極集中的凸起。$\sinh / \sin$ 轉換點就是 dB 圖上看到的轉折。

Q. Comparing β=2, β=6, β=10 Kaiser spectra, the first side-lobe sinks by roughly how much going from β=2 to β=10?

問：比較 β=2、β=6、β=10 的 Kaiser 頻譜，第一旁瓣從 β=2 到 β=10 大約下降多少？
