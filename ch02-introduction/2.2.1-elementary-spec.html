Any real sound is infinite in principle. To analyse it digitally we always do the same three things:

任何真實聲音原則上都是無限長。要用數位方式分析它，我們永遠都在做同樣的三件事：

  1. Cut out a finite chunk. Multiply by a window function. The window's shape controls how much spectral leakage we suffer.
  2. 切出一段有限長度。乘上一個視窗函數。 視窗形狀決定頻譜洩漏有多嚴重。
  3. Take a transform. DFT (sampled DTFT) turns samples into frequency-domain numbers. Fourier theorems (linearity, shift, convolution, Parseval, …) tell us how time-domain operations show up on the other side.
  4. 做一次轉換。DFT （採樣版的 DTFT）把樣本變成頻域數字。 傅立葉定理（線性、位移、卷積、Parseval…）告訴我們時域操作對應到頻域變成什麼。
  5. Apply a filter. If we want to reshape or isolate some band, we need to design an FIR whose impulse response produces the desired frequency response.
  6. 套一個濾波器。如果要重塑或只留下某個頻帶， 就要設計 FIR，讓它的脈衝響應對應到我們要的頻率響應。
DFT (length N)
$$X[k]=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[n]\,e^{-j 2\pi k n /N},\qquad k=0,1,\dots,N-1$$
