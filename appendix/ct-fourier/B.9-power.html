$\int |x(t)|^2\,dt = \tfrac{1}{2\pi}\int |X(\omega)|^2\,d\omega$. Total energy is preserved across FT — hence Fourier is unitary (up to scaling). More generally: $\int x\bar y\,dt=\tfrac{1}{2\pi}\int X\bar Y\,d\omega$.
