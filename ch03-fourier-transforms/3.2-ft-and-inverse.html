A round trip — analyse with the FT, rebuild with the inverse FT, and ask when the trip is even legal.

一趟來回 — 用 FT 分析、用反 FT 重建，再問問這條路本來就走得通嗎？

Forward and inverse

正轉換與反轉換

Discrete time, continuous frequency:

離散時間、連續頻率：

DTFT & INVERSE
$$X(\omega)\;=\;\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,e^{-j\omega n}$$
$$x[n]\;=\;\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}X(\omega)\,e^{j\omega n}\,d\omega$$

Continuous time, continuous frequency (for reference):

連續時間、連續頻率（對照用）：

CONTINUOUS-TIME FT & INVERSE
$$X(j\Omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x(t)\,e^{-j\Omega t}\,dt,\qquad x(t)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}X(j\Omega)\,e^{j\Omega t}\,d\Omega$$

When does the sum even converge?

什麼時候這個求和才會收斂？

Three increasingly forgiving categories. From strongest to weakest:

三種由嚴格到寬鬆的存在條件，由強到弱：

  1. Absolute summability : $\sum |x[n]|<\infty$. The sum converges uniformly and $X(\omega)$ is continuous. Most well-behaved signals (decaying transients, impulses, finite-length sequences) live here.
  2. 絕對可和： $\sum|x[n]|<\infty$。求和均勻收斂，$X(\omega)$ 連續。 大多數行為良好的訊號（衰減瞬態、脈衝、有限長序列）都在這層。
  3. Square summability : $\sum|x[n]|^2<\infty$. The sum converges in the mean-square sense; $X(\omega)$ might have isolated jumps.
  4. 平方可和： $\sum|x[n]|^2<\infty$。和以均方意義收斂；$X(\omega)$ 允許有零星跳動。
  5. Generalised functions : Even sequences that are not summable in the usual sense (constants, pure sinusoids, steps) get a Fourier transform — but it lives in the distribution sense, with Dirac deltas. This unifies "ideal" frequency-domain shapes with reality.
  6. 廣義函數：連一般意義下不可和的序列（常數、純正弦波、單位步階） 也能有傅立葉轉換 — 只是要用分布意義， 表示式中會出現 Dirac delta。 這讓「理想頻譜」與真實訊號在同一套理論下共存。

For practical work the rule of thumb is: finite-length signals always have a DTFT. We almost never run into convergence trouble because we are always windowing the data first.

實務上的口訣：有限長序列一定有 DTFT。 因為我們在分析前一定先加窗，幾乎不會撞上收斂問題。
